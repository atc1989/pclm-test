Concept What GLIS measures — and what it doesn't
The Specific Target

Inflammaging — chronic low-grade systemic inflammation

Sustained, sub-clinical inflammatory state driven by metabolic dysfunction, gut dysbiosis, lifestyle, and aging. CRP typically 1–10 mg/L.

Anchored in peer-reviewed literature: Franceschi 2000, Furman 2019 (Nat Med), Levine 2018 (Aging).

Exclusion Zone

When GLIS is the wrong tool

Acute infections · autoimmune disease (RA, SLE, IBD) · organ-specific inflammation · allergic / Type 2 inflammation · cancer-associated inflammation · neuroinflammation · pregnancy · pediatric (under 18) · established CV or diabetic disease management.

Operationally enforced via the patient-facing pre-scan check-in. Patients reporting these conditions at draw time route to Reschedule recommended.

Good to scan

Full confidence. No relevant conditions reported around blood draw date.

Scan with caveat
Mag-scan na, may note
Score with interpretive note. Vaccination 24–72hr before · NSAIDs/steroids/antibiotics · strenuous exercise within 48hr · alcohol 3+ within 72hr · poor sleep · non-fasted · acute stress within 7 days · active menstruation · non-AM draw (Complete).
→ Score computed normally with caveat note shown to patient and physician.
Reschedule recommended
Iurong muna ang scan
Score deferred. Active infection within 2 weeks · surgery within 4 weeks · active autoimmune flare · cancer treatment · pregnancy · vaccination within 24hr.

Decision Support Worked clinical cases
Case 1

Standard use, independent

Good to scan

A 42-year-old woman with a family history of type 2 diabetes presents reporting persistent fatigue. She uses GLIS independently (Pathway A). Her blood is drawn October 10 at 7:30 AM, fasted, after seven hours of sleep, with no recent illness or relevant medications.

Markers come back: hs-CRP 2.8, NLR 2.4, HbA1c 5.9%, ALT 28, eGFR 88. GLIS Core score: 58 (Pay Attention / Pansinin).

Decision Patient enrolls in protocol and decides to discuss her trending-prediabetic HbA1c with her own PCP at the next visit. She downloads the GLIS PDF report to share. Her PCP remains medical home; GutGuard tracks inflammation trajectory.
Case 2

Recent infection at draw time

Reschedule

A 35-year-old man uploads results 10 days post-recovery. His blood was drawn on October 8 at 8:00 AM. On the check-in question 1, he answers Yes — he was symptomatic with flu on the draw date.

Markers: hs-CRP 5.2, NLR 3.8, HbA1c 5.4%, ALT 22, eGFR 95. The system defers; individual markers remain visible but no composite score is computed.

Decision Markers reflect a recovering infection, not inflammaging. Patient sees individual values for reference and reschedules a new draw two weeks after symptom resolution. No physician action needed (Pathway A is self-directed). The temporal anchor caught what an “are you sick now?” question would have missed.
Case 3

Physician override, supervising pathway

Override

A 58-year-old man with Crohn’s in 18-month remission on infliximab is in Pathway C with you as supervising physician. On Q3 he answers Yes — interpreting “treated for an autoimmune condition” literally because he’s on biologic therapy.

The system recommends Reschedule (Tier 1 defer). But you have clinical context: calprotectin under 50 at the June check, no inflammatory symptoms in office visits before or after the draw.

Override Document justification — sustained remission, calprotectin under 50, no flare symptoms. System computes Plus 38 (Good / Maayos) with override annotation. Educate patient on Q3 phrasing for future scans. Audit trail captures the date-specific reasoning.
Case 4

Critical finding, independent pathway

Critical

A 51-year-old woman, asymptomatic at draw, uses Pathway A. Blood drawn November 5 at 7:00 AM; check-in answers all “No”; system says Good to scan.

Markers: hs-CRP 11.5, NLR 3.2, HbA1c 5.8%, ALT 24, eGFR 86. The severe-abnormal floor triggers — CRP > 10 — producing Take Action / Kumilos Na.

Acknowledgment soft-gate System displays: “hs-CRP 11.5 mg/L. Consult Internal Medicine before continuing protocol.” Patient checks acknowledgment, schedules PCP appointment. PCP workup identifies early Sjögren’s. PCP becomes primary clinician; GutGuard role secondary. This is the kind of finding GLIS surfaces but cannot diagnose.

What you should know

Validation pending. GLIS is based on peer-reviewed inflammaging literature, but the specific composite has not undergone independent peer-reviewed validation. v1.5 study planned: n ≥ 150 Filipino adults, IRB submission Q3 2026, target publication Philippine Journal of Internal Medicine.

Commercial conflict. GLIS is owned and operated by IG International / GutGuard, manufacturer of the Pre→Pro→Postbiotic + MBS protocol that GLIS is used to assess. Methodology safeguards: operationally enforced exclusion zone, peer-reviewed marker selection, transparent validation roadmap.

Until validation completes: Use GLIS as one component of clinical assessment, not as a standalone tool. Communicate to patients that GLIS is a screening tool with validation pending.
