Protocol Science
Clinical Reference
BioScan
GLIS · IMSI v1.0
Inflammatory and metabolic
pattern scoring
Computes a 0–100 GLIS score from your patient’s lab panel using the v1.3 methodology — targeting inflammaging (chronic low-grade systemic inflammation) across three biological domains: inflammatory (hs-CRP, NLR), metabolic (HbA1c), and organ function (ALT, eGFR). Plus and Complete tiers add ferritin, TyG, uric acid, albumin, homocysteine, fasting insulin/HOMA-IR, GGT, cortisol AM, and DHEA-S. Pre-scan check-in anchored to blood draw date gates score validity.
The gut drives systemic inflammation. The gut microbiome determines whether inflammatory markers rise or fall. BioScan scores the result — not the cause, but the measurable consequence.
The score is a clinical reference, not a directive.
You review the score, apply your clinical judgment, and determine the appropriate next step for your patient.
For monitoring purposes only. IMSI v1.0 does not diagnose, prescribe, or replace clinical evaluation.

Scoring Model
GLIS · IMSI v1.0
Inflammatory–Metabolic Markers Index
A composite biomarker-based index that estimates inflammatory and metabolic burden using routine laboratory markers, designed for monitoring trends and supporting clinical interpretation.
Patient-facing: Optimal / Good / Pay Attention / Take Action with Tagalog companion (Mabuti / Maayos / Pansinin / Kumilos Na)  ·  Brand: GutGuard GLIS (Gut-Lifestyle Inflammation Score) — targets inflammaging
Dual-Domain Model
IMSI = Inflammatory Domain (50%) + Metabolic Domain (50%)
INFLAMMATORY DOMAIN (50%)
hs-CRP (Primary anchor)
<1.0 = 0pts
1–3 = 10pts
3–5 = 15pts
>5 = 20pts
NLR (Immune-inflammatory balance)
<2.0 = 0pts
2–3 = 8pts
3–5 = 15pts
>5 = 20pts
Score = (CRP pts + NLR pts) / 40 × 50
METABOLIC DOMAIN (50%)
Core: Glucose (0–18) · Triglycerides (0–18) · HDL (0–12) · TyG Index (0–20)
Modifier: ALT (0–10)
TyG = ln((TG × Glucose) / 2)
Score = (Glu + TG + HDL + TyG + ALT) / 78 × 50

Full Disclosure

Optional supervising physician role. Participation as a GutGuard supervising physician is voluntary and opt-in. You can decline cases, set your own caseload limits, or withdraw from active supervision with 30 days’ notice. Your responsibility is limited to findings flagged by the system that you have reviewed, recommendations you have explicitly endorsed, and override decisions you have made with documented justification. Patients without a GutGuard supervising physician (Pathway A — the default) are entirely self-directed for clinical follow-up; you have no review obligation for those patients.

GLIS Score. The GLIS (Gut-Lifestyle Inflammation Score) is a composite biological score targeting inflammaging — chronic low-grade systemic inflammation. The methodology is documented in the GLIS Clinical Methodology v1.3 (April 2026), based on peer-reviewed inflammaging literature (Franceschi 2000, Furman 2019, Levine 2018). The specific composite has not yet undergone independent peer-reviewed validation; a validation study (n ≥ 150 Filipino adults, IRB submission Q3 2026, target publication Philippine Journal of Internal Medicine) is on the roadmap. GLIS is not a clinical diagnostic instrument and not a medical device under FDA Philippines regulations.

Pre-scan check-in gate. Before any score is computed, patients complete a 12-question check-in anchored to their blood draw date. The system issues one of three recommendations: Good to scan (Tier 3, full confidence), Scan with caveat (Tier 2, score with interpretive notes), or Reschedule recommended (Tier 1, score deferred). You can override Tier 1 deferrals with mandatory clinical justification logged in the audit trail.

Off-hours coverage. The 24-hour acknowledgment window for mandatory critical findings refers to business-day hours (Monday–Friday, 8 AM – 6 PM PHT). Critical findings flagged outside business hours have their acknowledgment clock starting the next business-day morning. Patients receive critical-finding notifications immediately regardless of physician availability and can seek consultation through emergency or urgent care channels independently.

Physician independence. Enrolled physicians are independent licensed professionals — not employees or agents of IG International / GutGuard. Physician involvement does not create a doctor–patient relationship with IG International / GutGuard (RA 2382). IG International / GutGuard does not practice medicine.

Supplement scope. GutGuard SynBIOTIC+ is a licensed lifestyle food supplement (FDA-PH CPR FR-4XXXXXXX · LTO FDO-XXXXXXX). Protocol assignment is a supplement recommendation — not a prescription or clinical order under RA 2382 or RA 9173.

Message templates. Message templates in this portal are optional starting points only. The enrolled physician is fully responsible for all communications sent under their PRC licence.

Data privacy. Patient data is processed under RA 10173 (Data Privacy Act). Enrolled physicians must obtain patient consent before submitting a BioScan and must not use patient data outside the GutGuard review workflow. The audit trail logs every BioScan upload, OCR session, blood draw date confirmation, pre-scan check-in responses, system recommendation, override decisions, and critical-finding acknowledgments — retained minimum 5 years.

Tax compliance. Protocol review credits redeemable via vRedeem constitute taxable income under the Philippine NIRC (BIR). Enrolled physicians are responsible for their own BIR compliance.
